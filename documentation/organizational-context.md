# Organizational Context

Kairos is a human-maintained trading-agent system. It monitors market-relevant information through human-authored laws and branch-specific agents.

Use this context only as a light relevance filter. Do not over-process content, over-classify it, or turn every detail into a durable memory. Prefer preserving useful facts, source context, and human feedback in a simple form.

## What Matters

Keep content when it helps Kairos remember:

- A law, branch, thesis, source preference, or ignore rule.
- A human correction, preference, or note about what should matter.
- A concrete market-relevant event involving a tracked company, asset, sector, or catalyst.
- Evidence that an event may be new, stale, duplicate, credible, unreliable, material, or too noisy to matter.
- A prior heartbeat trigger, escalation, debate, decision, false positive, missed event, or outcome.

## What Usually Does Not Matter

Ignore or downrank:

- Generic commentary with no concrete claim.
- Reposts, duplicates, stale summaries, and repeated boilerplate.
- Promotional content, hype, memes, ads, SEO filler, navigation text, and page clutter.
- Weak rumors unless they are useful as examples of low-quality or unverified sources.
- Broad macro commentary unless it clearly relates to a tracked law, asset, branch, or catalyst.

## How To Process Content

Keep processing minimal.

When something is worth remembering, store a concise memory that captures:

- What happened.
- Which law, branch, ticker, company, sector, or debate it relates to, if known.
- The source and timestamp, if available.
- Whether the information is official, reported, human-provided, model-generated, inferred, or unverified.
- Why it may matter, if that is clear from the content.

Do not invent conclusions. Do not convert content into trade instructions. Do not treat human notes as automatic truth. Preserve uncertainty when the source is unclear, disputed, or incomplete.

## Style

Stored memories should be short, factual, and easy for future heartbeat, gate, research, and debate agents to retrieve.

Good:

> Human correction: for PLTR deal laws, ignore reseller announcements unless end customer, contract value, or strategic expansion is clear.

Good:

> PLTR branch `government_deals` escalated on 2026-05-03 after an official NHS contract notice suggested a new multi-year deployment; materiality was uncertain because contract value was not yet known.

Bad:

> Interesting Palantir news.
